# SPICE CN / CN+S Apparent-LOS Cross-Validation

_Generated by `examples/spice_validate_cn_cnplus.py` (validation only; the measurement model is unchanged)._

## Method

The fixture truth trajectory is written to a temporary type-13 (Hermite) SPK as a custom body (id -999) centred on the Moon in J2000, densely sampled from the model interpolant `interp_state_history`. SPICE then evaluates the **same** trajectory from each Earth station with `spkcpo(..., abcorr, obsctr='EARTH', obsref='ITRF93')`, returning the apparent line of sight in **J2000 axes** (station -> spacecraft):

- **NONE** — geometric, no correction (`abcorr='NONE'`): isolates the SPK / frame / station setup.

- **CN**  — converged Newtonian one-way light time (`abcorr='CN'`).

- **CN+S** — CN plus stellar aberration (`abcorr='CN+S'`), whose reception-case observer velocity is relative to the SSB — matching our `spice_ssb` model.

## 3. Diagnosis — the CN difference is the Moon's light-time displacement

The CN difference equals, to within metres, the distance the **Moon moves (relative to the SSB) during the one-way light time**. Our model forms the light-time LOS in the **Moon-centred (MCI) frame**, treating it as inertial; SPICE forms it in the SSB-inertial frame. Because the Moon travels at ~30 km/s w.r.t. the SSB, over the ~1.36 s Earth-Moon light time it moves ~40 km, and `LOS_MCI - LOS_SSB = Moon(t_r) - Moon(t_r - τ)`.

## Conclusions

1. **Harness is exact** — geometric (NONE) LOS matches SPICE to 1.3e-07 m, so the comparison is trustworthy.

2. **Finding: the model's light-time LOS is built in the Moon-centred (non-inertial) frame.** It differs from SPICE's SSB-inertial CN by the Moon's barycentric displacement during the light time — up to 4.0e+04 m of LOS / 1.2e-04 s of light time / 10.3" of direction. This is a **frame approximation, not a light-time iteration error** (the iteration itself converges; the offset is exactly the Moon's motion, ratio ≈ 1.000).

3. **Self-consistent, so synthetic OD is unbiased.** Generation and residual prediction use the same MCI-frame computation, so the offset cancels in observed-minus-computed; it would matter only for absolute fidelity against real DSN data or SPICE-referenced ephemerides.

4. **The stellar-aberration rotation is correct** — the CN->CN+S shift matches SPICE to 8.85e-04 arcsec; the CN+S difference simply inherits the CN frame offset.


_Recommendation (not applied here, per the validation-only scope): to be inertial-exact against SPICE/real data, form the light-time LOS in an inertial frame — e.g. add the Moon's `(t_r) - (t_r - τ)` displacement, or reference the spacecraft and station to a common SSB/J2000 origin before differencing._
